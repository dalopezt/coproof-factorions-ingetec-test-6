**Theorem.** The set of natural numbers equal to the sum of the factorials of their own decimal digits is exactly $\{1, 2, 145, 40585\}$. That is, defining

$$f(n) = \sum_{d \,\in\, \mathrm{digits}_{10}(n)} d!$$

where $\mathrm{digits}_{10}(n)$ denotes the multiset of base-10 digits of $n$, we have

$$\{n \in \mathbb{N} \mid f(n) = n\} = \{1,\, 2,\, 145,\, 40585\}.$$
